ACT: Indian Contract Act, 1872
SECTION: 73
TITLE: Compensation for Loss or Damage Caused by Breach of Contract
CATEGORY: Contract Law - Remedies for Breach
---
Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act provides the framework for awarding compensation for loss or damage caused by breach of contract. When a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for such breach, such amount as would in the ordinary course of things flow from the breach (general damages), or which the parties knew at the time of making the contract to be likely to result from the breach (special damages). The section further provides that compensation is not to be given for any remote and indirect loss or damage sustained by reason of the breach. This provision is closely aligned with the rule in Hadley v. Baxendale (1854) of English law, incorporating both limbs: damages arising naturally from the breach and damages within the contemplation of the parties. The Explanation to Section 73 clarifies that in estimating damages, the means which existed of remedying the inconvenience caused by non-performance must be taken into account, essentially codifying the duty to mitigate losses. The Supreme Court in ONGC v. Saw Pipes Ltd. (2003) held that if the damages assessed by the court under Section 73 are found to be reasonable, the court can award such amount even if it exceeds the amount specified in a liquidated damages clause under Section 74. Compensation under Section 73 must be proved with reasonable certainty; speculative or hypothetical damages are not recoverable. The party claiming damages bears the burden of proving the actual loss suffered and its causal connection with the breach.
