ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 306
TITLE: Abetment of Suicide
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Body
---
Section 306 of the Indian Penal Code provides that if any person commits suicide, whoever abets the commission of such suicide shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall also be liable to fine. The concept of abetment is defined under Section 107 and includes instigating, engaging in a conspiracy, or intentionally aiding the commission of the offence. For a conviction under this section, the prosecution must establish that the deceased committed suicide, and that the accused abetted the commission of that suicide through active instigation or intentional aid. The Supreme Court in Chitresh Kumar Chopra v. State (2009) held that the accused must have played an active role in inciting the deceased to commit suicide, and mere allegation of harassment without evidence of direct nexus to the suicidal act is insufficient. In cases involving married women, Section 306 is frequently invoked alongside Section 498A (cruelty) and Section 304B (dowry death), particularly where suicide occurs within seven years of marriage due to harassment for dowry. The distinction between abetment of suicide (Section 306) and dowry death (Section 304B) lies in the nature of evidence required — Section 304B creates a presumption when death occurs within seven years of marriage in connection with dowry demand. A suicide note, while relevant, is not conclusive proof and must be corroborated with other evidence. The Madras High Court and other courts have clarified that mere quarrel or misunderstanding between spouses does not amount to abetment of suicide.
