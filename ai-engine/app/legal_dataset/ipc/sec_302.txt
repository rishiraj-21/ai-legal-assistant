ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 302
TITLE: Punishment for Murder
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Body
---
Section 302 of the Indian Penal Code prescribes the punishment for the offence of murder as defined under Section 300. Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine. The death penalty is awarded only in the "rarest of rare" cases, a principle established by the Supreme Court in Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab (1980). For a conviction under Section 302, the prosecution must establish beyond reasonable doubt that the accused caused the death of another person with the intention of causing death, or with the intention of causing such bodily injury as the accused knew to be likely to cause death, or with the intention of causing bodily injury sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death. The distinction between Section 302 (murder) and Section 304 (culpable homicide not amounting to murder) often turns on the degree of intention and the nature of the act. Courts examine the weapon used, the number of blows, the part of the body targeted, and the circumstances leading to the act to determine whether the case falls under murder or culpable homicide. The burden of proof lies entirely on the prosecution, and the accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty. Circumstantial evidence, if relied upon, must form a complete chain leaving no reasonable ground for acquittal.
