ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 323
TITLE: Punishment for Voluntarily Causing Hurt
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Body
---
Section 323 of the Indian Penal Code prescribes the punishment for voluntarily causing hurt as defined under Section 319. Whoever, except in the case provided by Section 334 (hurt caused on grave and sudden provocation), voluntarily causes hurt shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to one year, or with fine which may extend to one thousand rupees, or with both. "Hurt" under Section 319 is defined as causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity to any person. The offence under Section 323 is cognizable, bailable, and compoundable by the person to whom hurt is caused with the permission of the court. This section covers relatively minor physical injuries such as slapping, punching, pushing, or any act causing bodily pain that does not amount to "grievous hurt" as defined under Section 320. If the hurt caused is grievous — including emasculation, permanent privation of sight or hearing, fracture or dislocation of bone, or any hurt endangering life — the offence is punishable under Section 325 with up to seven years imprisonment. Section 323 is one of the most commonly invoked provisions in criminal law, frequently used in cases arising from domestic disputes, neighbourhood quarrels, road rage incidents, and workplace altercations. Courts typically consider the medical evidence, the nature of injuries, the weapon used, and witness testimony to determine whether the hurt falls under Section 323 or the graver Section 325.
