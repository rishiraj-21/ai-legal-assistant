ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 304
TITLE: Punishment for Culpable Homicide not amounting to Murder
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Body
---
Section 304 of the Indian Penal Code deals with the punishment for culpable homicide not amounting to murder, as defined under Section 299 read with the exceptions in Section 300. The section is divided into two parts: Part I applies where the act is done with the intention of causing death or of causing such bodily injury as is likely to cause death, punishable with imprisonment for life or imprisonment up to ten years and fine. Part II applies where the act is done with the knowledge that it is likely to cause death but without any intention to cause death or bodily injury, punishable with imprisonment up to ten years, or fine, or both. The key distinction between murder (Section 302) and culpable homicide (Section 304) lies in the degree of intention and knowledge — murder requires a higher degree of intention. The five exceptions to Section 300, including grave and sudden provocation, private defence exceeding the right, public servant exceeding powers, sudden fight without premeditation, and death by consent of the victim above 18 years, reduce murder to culpable homicide not amounting to murder. The Supreme Court in K.M. Nanavati v. State of Maharashtra (1962) extensively discussed the applicability of Exception 1 (grave and sudden provocation). The burden of proving the applicability of exceptions rests on the accused on a balance of probabilities, not beyond reasonable doubt.
