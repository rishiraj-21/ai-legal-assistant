ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 498A
TITLE: Husband or Relative of Husband of a Woman Subjecting Her to Cruelty
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Women
---
Section 498A of the Indian Penal Code, inserted by the Criminal Law (Second Amendment) Act of 1983, makes it an offence for the husband or any relative of the husband to subject a woman to cruelty. The punishment prescribed is imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine. The term "cruelty" under Explanation (a) includes any wilful conduct which is of such a nature as is likely to drive the woman to commit suicide or to cause grave injury or danger to life, limb, or health (whether mental or physical) of the woman. Explanation (b) covers harassment of the woman with a view to coercing her or any person related to her to meet any unlawful demand for property or valuable security, or on account of failure to meet such demand. The offence is cognizable, non-bailable, and non-compoundable, though the Supreme Court in Rajesh Sharma v. State of U.P. (2017) and B.S. Joshi v. State of Haryana (2003) allowed quashing of proceedings under Section 482 CrPC if parties have settled. In Arnesh Kumar v. State of Bihar (2014), the Supreme Court issued guidelines restricting automatic arrest under Section 498A, mandating that police first ascertain whether arrest is necessary. The section is often invoked alongside Section 304B (dowry death) and Section 406 (criminal breach of trust for stridhan). Despite concerns about misuse, the Supreme Court in Sushil Kumar Sharma v. Union of India (2005) upheld its constitutional validity, noting that the possibility of misuse does not invalidate a provision.
