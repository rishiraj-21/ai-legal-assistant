ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 406
TITLE: Punishment for Criminal Breach of Trust
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Property
---
Section 406 of the Indian Penal Code prescribes the punishment for criminal breach of trust as defined under Section 405. Whoever commits criminal breach of trust shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both. Criminal breach of trust occurs when a person who is entrusted with property or dominion over property dishonestly misappropriates or converts to his own use that property, or dishonestly uses or disposes of that property in violation of the legal contract or any direction of law prescribing the mode of trust. The essential ingredients are: entrustment of property, the person entrusted must be in a position of trust, and there must be dishonest misappropriation or conversion. This section is commonly invoked in cases involving employees misappropriating funds, partners diverting partnership assets, agents misusing principal's property, and trustees breaching fiduciary duties. Aggravated forms are punished under Sections 407 (by carrier, wharfinger, etc.), 408 (by clerk or servant), and 409 (by public servant, banker, merchant, or agent), carrying higher sentences up to life imprisonment. The Supreme Court in Pratibha Rani v. Suraj Kumar (1985) held that stridhan entrusted to the husband amounts to entrustment under this section. For prosecution, the complaint must establish a clear relationship of entrustment and subsequent dishonest dealing with the property.
