ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 354
TITLE: Assault or Criminal Force to Woman with Intent to Outrage Her Modesty
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Women
---
Section 354 of the Indian Penal Code provides that whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than one year but which may extend to five years, and shall also be liable to fine. The offence is cognizable, non-bailable, and triable by any Magistrate. The term "modesty" has been interpreted by the Supreme Court in Rupan Deol Bajaj v. KPS Gill (1995) to mean the essence of a woman's femininity, and any act that can be perceived as an affront to the normal sense of female decency is an outrage to her modesty. The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013 introduced sub-sections 354A (sexual harassment), 354B (assault with intent to disrobe), 354C (voyeurism), and 354D (stalking) to comprehensively address various forms of sexual offences against women. The prosecution must prove both the use of criminal force or assault and the intention or knowledge of outraging the woman's modesty. The testimony of the victim is given significant weight, and corroboration, while desirable, is not an absolute requirement for conviction. This section is frequently invoked alongside other provisions such as Section 509 (word, gesture, or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman).
