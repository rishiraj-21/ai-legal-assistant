ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 376
TITLE: Punishment for Rape
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Offences Against Women
---
Section 376 of the Indian Penal Code prescribes the punishment for the offence of rape as defined under Section 375. The minimum punishment is rigorous imprisonment for a term not less than ten years, which may extend to imprisonment for life, and the convict shall also be liable to fine. In cases of aggravated rape under Section 376(2) — including rape by a police officer, public servant, member of armed forces, relative or guardian, or gang rape — the minimum punishment is rigorous imprisonment for not less than ten years extendable to life imprisonment meaning imprisonment for the remainder of the person's natural life. The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013, enacted after the Nirbhaya case, significantly expanded the definition of rape under Section 375 to include penetration by any body part or object, and removed the requirement of penile-vaginal penetration alone. Section 376A provides for punishment of death or imprisonment for not less than twenty years where the offence causes death or results in a persistent vegetative state. The testimony of the prosecutrix (victim) is treated as that of an injured witness and can sustain a conviction without corroboration if it inspires confidence. The concept of marital rape exception remains under Section 375 Exception 2, exempting sexual intercourse by a man with his own wife above 18 years, though this has been subject to constitutional challenges. The Supreme Court in State of Punjab v. Gurmit Singh (1996) held that courts should not question the character of the victim or subject her to humiliation during cross-examination.
