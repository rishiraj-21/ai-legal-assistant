ACT: Indian Penal Code, 1860
SECTION: 504
TITLE: Intentional Insult with Intent to Provoke Breach of the Peace
CATEGORY: Criminal Law - Criminal Intimidation and Insult
---
Section 504 of the Indian Penal Code provides that whoever intentionally insults, and thereby gives provocation to any person, intending or knowing it to be likely that such provocation will cause him to break the public peace or to commit any other offence, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both. The essential ingredients of this offence are: the accused must have intentionally insulted the complainant, the insult must have been intended to or was likely to provoke the complainant, and the provocation must be of a nature that could lead to breach of public peace or commission of another offence. The offence is cognizable, bailable, and compoundable by the person insulted with the permission of the court. The Supreme Court has held that mere abuse or use of filthy language in a quarrel does not automatically attract Section 504 unless it is shown that the accused intended to provoke the other person to break the public peace. In Balobal Suri v. State of Rajasthan, it was observed that the word "insult" must be construed having regard to the status, position, and circumstances of the parties involved. Section 504 is frequently charged alongside Section 506 (criminal intimidation), Section 323 (voluntarily causing hurt), and Section 34 (common intention) in cases involving altercations and public disputes. The prosecution must prove that the insult was deliberate and calculated, not merely the result of a heated exchange where both parties traded words equally.
