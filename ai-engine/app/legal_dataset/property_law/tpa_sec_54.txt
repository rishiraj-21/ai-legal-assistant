ACT: Transfer of Property Act, 1882
SECTION: 54
TITLE: Sale Defined
CATEGORY: Property Law - Transfer by Sale
---
Section 54 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 defines "sale" as a transfer of ownership in exchange for a price paid or promised or part-paid and part-promised. The section distinguishes between sale of tangible immovable property of the value of one hundred rupees and upwards (or in the case of a simple mortgage of such property), which can only be made by a registered instrument, and sale of tangible immovable property of value less than one hundred rupees, which may be made either by a registered instrument or by delivery of the property. A contract for the sale of immovable property is a contract that a sale of such property shall take place on terms settled between the parties, and such a contract does not of itself create any interest in or charge on the property. The essential elements of a valid sale are: the parties must be competent to transfer and receive, the property must be transferable, the transfer must be for a price (money consideration), and the transfer must be completed by registration where required. The Supreme Court in Suraj Lamp & Industries Pvt. Ltd. v. State of Haryana (2012) held that sale agreements accompanied by general power of attorney and will (the GPA-Will-Sale route) do not convey title and that immovable property can only be legally transferred by a registered sale deed. Section 54 read with Section 55 imposes duties on the seller including disclosure of material defects, production of title documents, payment of outgoings up to the date of sale, and delivery of possession. The buyer is entitled to the benefit of any improvement or accession to the property between the date of the contract and the transfer of ownership. Registration of the sale deed is mandatory under the Registration Act, 1908 for immovable property valued above one hundred rupees.
