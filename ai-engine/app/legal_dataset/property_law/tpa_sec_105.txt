ACT: Transfer of Property Act, 1882
SECTION: 105
TITLE: Lease Defined
CATEGORY: Property Law - Lease
---
Section 105 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 defines a "lease" of immovable property as a transfer of a right to enjoy such property, made for a certain time, express or implied, or in perpetuity, in consideration of a price paid or promised, or of money, a share of crops, service, or any other thing of value, to be rendered periodically or on specified occasions to the transferor by the transferee, who accepts the transfer on such terms. The transferor is called the lessor, the transferee the lessee, the price is called the premium, and the money, share, service, or other thing to be rendered is called the rent. A lease of immovable property from year to year, or for any term exceeding one year, or reserving a yearly rent, can be made only by a registered instrument under Section 107. A lease for a period not exceeding one year may be made either by a registered instrument or by oral agreement accompanied by delivery of possession. The rights and liabilities of the lessor and lessee are enumerated in Sections 108 and 109, including the lessor's obligation to disclose material defects and put the lessee in possession, and the lessee's obligation to pay rent, maintain the property, and restore it upon termination. Section 111 specifies the grounds for termination of lease including efflux of time, happening of a specified event, merger, express surrender, implied surrender, forfeiture, and expiry of notice to quit. The lessee has a statutory right to remove fixtures attached to the property for trade, manufacture, or ornament during the lease or within a reasonable period after termination. Lease agreements are subject to state-specific Rent Control Acts which may override certain provisions of the Transfer of Property Act, particularly regarding eviction and rent fixation.
