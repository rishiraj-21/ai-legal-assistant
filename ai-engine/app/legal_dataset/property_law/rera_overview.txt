ACT: Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016
SECTION: Overview
TITLE: RERA 2016 - Comprehensive Overview
CATEGORY: Property Law - Real Estate Regulation
---
The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) was enacted to regulate and promote the real estate sector, ensure sale of plots, apartments, or buildings in an efficient and transparent manner, and protect the interest of consumers. The Act mandates registration of all real estate projects where the land exceeds 500 square meters or where more than eight apartments are proposed with the Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) established by each state under Section 20. Section 3 requires every promoter to register the real estate project before advertising, marketing, booking, selling, or offering for sale any plot, apartment, or building. The promoter is required to disclose on the RERA website: project details, layout plan, plan of development works, land title status, schedule of proposed completion, type of materials used, and names and addresses of real estate agents. Section 18 provides that if the promoter fails to complete the project or deliver possession on time, the allottee is entitled to a full refund with interest or may accept delayed delivery with interest compensation for every month of delay. Section 14 prohibits the promoter from transferring the majority rights in the project to a third party without the prior written consent of two-thirds of allottees and prior written approval of the Authority. The Real Estate Appellate Tribunal hears appeals from the Authority's orders under Section 43. Penalties for non-registration range up to ten percent of the estimated project cost, and continued violation may result in imprisonment up to three years. RERA has been held to be a beneficial legislation to be interpreted broadly in favour of allottees, and its provisions prevail over the terms of the builder-buyer agreement to the extent of any inconsistency.
