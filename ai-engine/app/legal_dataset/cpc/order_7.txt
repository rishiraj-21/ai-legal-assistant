ACT: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908
SECTION: Order VII
TITLE: Plaint
CATEGORY: Civil Procedure - Pleadings
---
Order VII of the Code of Civil Procedure governs the requirements for filing a plaint, which is the written statement by which a plaintiff initiates a civil suit. Rule 1 mandates that every plaint shall contain the following particulars: the name of the court, the name, description, and place of residence of the plaintiff and defendant, the facts constituting the cause of action and when it arose, the jurisdiction of the court, the relief claimed, and the amount of the claim where applicable. Rule 10 provides that a suit shall be returned for presentation to the court in which it should have been filed if the court finds it has no jurisdiction. Rule 11 is particularly significant as it empowers the court to reject a plaint on grounds including: where it does not disclose a cause of action, where the relief claimed is undervalued and the plaintiff fails to correct the valuation, where the plaint is written on insufficiently stamped paper, or where the suit appears barred by any law. The Supreme Court in Sopan Sukhdeo Sable v. Assistant Charity Commissioner (2004) held that the court must look at the averments in the plaint as a whole to determine if a cause of action is disclosed, and cannot examine the defence at the stage of Order VII Rule 11. Order VII Rule 14 deals with the production of documents on which the plaintiff sues or relies, requiring that every document in the plaintiff's possession or power be produced and filed with the plaint. Failure to comply with Order VII requirements can result in rejection of the plaint, though courts generally allow reasonable opportunities to cure defects before taking such drastic action.
