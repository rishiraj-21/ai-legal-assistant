ACT: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908
SECTION: Order IX
TITLE: Appearance of Parties and Consequence of Non-Appearance
CATEGORY: Civil Procedure - Appearance and Default
---
Order IX of the Code of Civil Procedure deals with the appearance of parties before the court and the consequences of their non-appearance. Rule 2 provides that where on the date fixed for hearing, the plaintiff appears but the defendant does not appear, the court may proceed ex parte against the defendant if satisfied that summons was duly served. Rule 3 states that where the plaintiff does not appear on the date fixed, the court shall dismiss the suit, unless the defendant admits the claim or part thereof. Rule 6 empowers the defendant against whom an ex parte decree has been passed to apply to the court for setting aside the decree, provided the defendant satisfies the court that summons was not duly served or that he was prevented by sufficient cause from appearing. Rule 7 provides that where the court finds it has been satisfied that summons was served and no sufficient cause is shown, the court shall reject the application for setting aside the ex parte decree. Rule 9 deals with the restoration of a suit dismissed for default of appearance of the plaintiff, allowing the plaintiff to apply for restoration if sufficient cause is shown for non-appearance. The Supreme Court in Parimal v. Veena (2011) emphasized that the power to set aside ex parte decrees should be exercised liberally, as courts exist to do justice and technical objections should not defeat substantive rights. The court must balance the principles of natural justice with the need for finality in litigation when considering applications under this Order.
