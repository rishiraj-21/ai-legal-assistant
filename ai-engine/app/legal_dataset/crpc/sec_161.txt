ACT: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
SECTION: 161
TITLE: Examination of Witnesses by Police
CATEGORY: Criminal Procedure - Investigation
---
Section 161 of the Code of Criminal Procedure deals with the examination of witnesses by police officers during the course of investigation. Sub-section (1) empowers any police officer making an investigation to examine orally any person supposed to be acquainted with the facts and circumstances of the case. Sub-section (2) provides that such person shall be bound to answer truly all questions relating to the case put to him by the police officer, other than questions the answers to which would have a tendency to expose the person to a criminal charge, penalty, or forfeiture. Sub-section (3) mandates that the police officer may reduce into writing any statement made to him during examination, and if it is reduced to writing, the person making the statement shall not be asked to sign it. Statements recorded under Section 161 are not substantive evidence and cannot be used as evidence in chief during trial. However, under Section 145 of the Indian Evidence Act, such statements can be used to contradict the witness during cross-examination, and under Section 157, to corroborate testimony given in court. The Supreme Court in State of U.P. v. M.K. Anthony (1985) clarified the procedure for using Section 161 statements for contradiction. These statements hold significant importance in criminal trials as they represent the first account given by witnesses to the investigating officer and can reveal inconsistencies if the witness changes their version at trial. The investigating officer has a duty to record statements fairly and accurately, and any material omission or improvement can be brought out during trial to test credibility.
