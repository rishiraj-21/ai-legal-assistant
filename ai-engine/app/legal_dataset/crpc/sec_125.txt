ACT: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
SECTION: 125
TITLE: Order for Maintenance of Wives, Children and Parents
CATEGORY: Criminal Procedure - Maintenance
---
Section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure provides for the maintenance of wives, children, and parents who are unable to maintain themselves. If any person having sufficient means neglects or refuses to maintain his wife, unable to maintain herself, or his legitimate or illegitimate minor child, or his legitimate or illegitimate child who has attained majority but is unable to maintain itself by reason of physical or mental abnormality, or his father or mother unable to maintain themselves, a Magistrate of the first class may order such person to make a monthly allowance for maintenance. The maximum maintenance that could be awarded was previously capped at Rs. 500 per month, but this ceiling was removed by the 2001 amendment, and now the Magistrate can award any amount deemed reasonable. The Supreme Court in Rajnesh v. Neha (2021) laid down comprehensive guidelines for maintenance proceedings, including the requirement of filing affidavits of income and assets by both parties. A wife is entitled to maintenance even if she is living separately, provided she establishes that she has sufficient reason for doing so, such as cruelty, desertion, or the husband marrying another woman. The term "wife" includes a divorced wife who has not remarried, as clarified by Explanation (b). Section 125 is applicable to all persons irrespective of religion, as it is a secular provision in the criminal law. The Magistrate may also order interim maintenance pending final disposal of the application, and wilful non-compliance with the maintenance order can result in execution proceedings including arrest and detention.
