ACT: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
SECTION: 438
TITLE: Direction for Grant of Bail to Person Apprehending Arrest (Anticipatory Bail)
CATEGORY: Criminal Procedure - Bail
---
Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure provides for the grant of anticipatory bail, which is a direction to release a person on bail in the event of his arrest for a non-bailable offence. Sub-section (1) states that where any person has reason to believe that he may be arrested on accusation of having committed a non-bailable offence, he may apply to the High Court or the Court of Session for a direction that in the event of such arrest, he shall be released on bail. The court may impose conditions on the grant of anticipatory bail, including requiring the person to make himself available for interrogation, not to make inducement, threat, or promise to any person acquainted with the facts, not to leave India without prior permission, and such other conditions as may be imposed under Section 437(3). The Supreme Court in Sushila Aggarwal v. State (NCT of Delhi) (2020) held that anticipatory bail can be granted without any time limit and need not be limited to a fixed period, overruling the earlier contrary view in Siddharam Satlingappa Mhetre v. State of Maharashtra (2011) on this specific point. The nature and gravity of the accusation, the antecedents of the applicant, the possibility of the applicant fleeing from justice, and the likelihood of the applicant tampering with evidence are factors the court considers. Anticipatory bail is not available in all states uniformly — for instance, Uttar Pradesh excluded its application through a state amendment until the amendment was struck down. The Supreme Court in Arnab Manoranjan Goswami v. State of Maharashtra (2020) emphasized that courts should not be niggardly in granting anticipatory bail when the case warrants it, as personal liberty under Article 21 is a fundamental right.
