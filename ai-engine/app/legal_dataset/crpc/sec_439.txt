ACT: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
SECTION: 439
TITLE: Special Powers of High Court or Court of Session Regarding Bail
CATEGORY: Criminal Procedure - Bail
---
Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure confers special powers on the High Court and the Court of Session to grant bail in cases where a Magistrate is not competent to grant bail under Section 437. Sub-section (1) empowers the High Court or Court of Session to direct that any person accused of an offence and in custody be released on bail, and to impose any condition which it considers necessary. Sub-section (2) empowers these courts to direct that any person who has been released on bail be arrested and committed to custody if the conditions of bail are violated. The Supreme Court in Satender Kumar Antil v. CBI (2022) laid down comprehensive guidelines for bail, categorizing offences and specifying when bail should be the norm rather than the exception, reinforcing the principle that "bail is the rule, jail is the exception." In Dataram Singh v. State of U.P. (2018), the Supreme Court identified the factors to be considered while granting bail: nature and gravity of charges, severity of punishment, danger of absconding, character and behaviour of the accused, likelihood of repetition of the offence, and possibility of tampering with evidence or influencing witnesses. The court granting bail under Section 439 has wider discretion than the Magistrate under Section 437 and can grant bail even in offences punishable with death or life imprisonment. However, bail in such grave offences requires the court to record special reasons. The High Court under Section 439(2) can also cancel bail if the accused misuses the liberty granted, and the power to cancel bail can be exercised on the application of the complainant or the prosecution.
