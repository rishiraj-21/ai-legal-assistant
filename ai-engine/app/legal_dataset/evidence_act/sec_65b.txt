ACT: Indian Evidence Act, 1872
SECTION: 65B
TITLE: Admissibility of Electronic Records
CATEGORY: Evidence Law - Electronic Evidence
---
Section 65B of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, inserted by the Information Technology Act, 2000, governs the admissibility of electronic records as evidence. Sub-section (1) provides that any information contained in an electronic record which is printed on paper, stored, recorded, or copied in optical or magnetic media produced by a computer shall be deemed to be also a document and shall be admissible in any proceedings without further proof or production of the original, if the conditions mentioned in sub-section (2) are satisfied. Sub-section (2) requires that the computer output containing the information was produced during the period over which the computer was used regularly, that the information was regularly fed into the computer in the ordinary course of activities, that the computer was operating properly during the relevant period, and that the information reproduces or is derived from information fed into the computer in the ordinary course. Sub-section (4) mandates that a certificate identifying the electronic record and describing the manner of its production shall accompany the electronic record, signed by a person occupying a responsible official position in relation to the operation of the relevant device or the management of the relevant activities. The Supreme Court in Arjun Panditrao Khotkar v. Kailash Kushanrao Gorantyal (2020) held that the certificate under Section 65B(4) is a mandatory condition for admissibility of electronic evidence, overruling the earlier decision in Shafhi Mohammad v. State of Himachal Pradesh (2018) which had relaxed this requirement. The court may also direct production of electronic evidence in the original form if the party producing the evidence has the original device. Electronic records include emails, text messages, social media posts, CCTV footage, call records, and any other computer-generated evidence.
