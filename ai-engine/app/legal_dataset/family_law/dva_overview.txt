ACT: Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005
SECTION: Overview
TITLE: Domestic Violence Act 2005 - Comprehensive Overview
CATEGORY: Family Law - Domestic Violence
---
The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (DV Act) provides civil remedies to women who are victims of domestic violence, defining domestic violence broadly to include physical abuse, sexual abuse, verbal and emotional abuse, and economic abuse. The Act protects any woman in a domestic relationship, including wives, live-in partners, mothers, sisters, and other female relatives residing in a shared household. Section 3 defines "domestic violence" comprehensively, covering acts of physical violence, threats, harassment for dowry demands, injury or harm causing physical or mental health impairment, and any act that has the effect of threatening the aggrieved person or any related person. The key reliefs available under the Act include: protection orders under Section 18 (restraining the respondent from committing domestic violence), residence orders under Section 19 (preventing dispossession from the shared household), monetary reliefs under Section 20 (covering loss of earnings, medical expenses, damage to property, and maintenance), custody orders under Section 21, and compensation orders under Section 22 (for mental torture and emotional distress). The concept of "shared household" under Section 2(s) has been given a wide interpretation by the Supreme Court in S.R. Batra v. Taruna Batra (2007) and later expanded in Satish Chander Ahuja v. Sneha Ahuja (2020) to include any household where the aggrieved person has lived in a domestic relationship. The Act provides for appointment of Protection Officers and registration of service providers to assist aggrieved women. Breach of a protection order is a cognizable and non-bailable offence punishable with imprisonment up to one year or fine up to twenty thousand rupees or both. Applications under the DV Act must be disposed of within sixty days from the date of first hearing.
