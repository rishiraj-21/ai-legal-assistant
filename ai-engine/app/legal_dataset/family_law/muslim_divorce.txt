ACT: Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986
SECTION: Overview
TITLE: Muslim Women Protection of Rights on Divorce - Comprehensive Overview
CATEGORY: Family Law - Muslim Personal Law
---
The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986 was enacted following the Supreme Court's landmark decision in Mohd. Ahmed Khan v. Shah Bano Begum (1985), which had held that a divorced Muslim woman is entitled to maintenance under Section 125 CrPC. The Act provides that a divorced Muslim woman is entitled to a reasonable and fair provision and maintenance within the iddat period (approximately three months after divorce) from her former husband under Section 3(1)(a). She is also entitled to maintenance for children born before or after divorce for a period of two years from the date of birth of such children under Section 3(1)(b), and the return of mahr (dower) or any other properties given to her by the husband, relatives, friends, or the husband's relatives under Section 3(1)(c) and (d). The Supreme Court in Danial Latifi v. Union of India (2001) interpreted the Act to mean that the obligation of the husband is not limited to the iddat period alone, but that a reasonable and fair provision must be made which extends beyond the iddat period and should be sufficient for the divorced woman's entire life unless she remarries. If the divorced woman is unable to maintain herself after the iddat period and has not remarried, Section 4 provides that the Magistrate may order her relatives who would inherit her property to pay maintenance, or in the absence of such relatives, the State Wakf Board. The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019 subsequently criminalized instant triple talaq (talaq-e-biddat), making it a cognizable offence punishable with imprisonment up to three years. The combined effect of these legislations and judicial interpretations ensures that divorced Muslim women have comprehensive legal protection regarding maintenance, property rights, and protection from arbitrary divorce.
