ACT: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955
SECTION: 24
TITLE: Maintenance Pendente Lite and Expenses of Proceedings
CATEGORY: Family Law - Maintenance
---
Section 24 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 provides for maintenance pendente lite (during the pendency of proceedings) and expenses of proceedings. It states that where in any proceeding under the Act it appears to the court that either the wife or the husband, as the case may be, has no independent income sufficient for her or his support and the necessary expenses of the proceeding, the court may on the application of the wife or husband order the respondent to pay to the petitioner the expenses of the proceeding, and monthly during the proceeding such sum as having regard to the petitioner's own income and the income of the respondent may be considered reasonable. The application for interim maintenance can be filed at any stage of the proceedings, including during appeal. The purpose of Section 24 is to ensure that the economically weaker spouse is not handicapped in pursuing or defending matrimonial litigation due to financial constraints. The Supreme Court in Rajnesh v. Neha (2021) laid down comprehensive guidelines for maintenance applications, mandating disclosure of income and assets through affidavits by both parties and directing that interim maintenance applications be decided within four to six months. Courts typically consider factors including the income and assets of both parties, the standard of living during the marriage, the reasonable needs of the applicant, the educational qualifications and earning capacity of both spouses, and the number of dependents. The maintenance awarded under Section 24 is temporary and ceases upon the final disposal of the proceedings, after which permanent alimony under Section 25 may be granted. Non-payment of maintenance can be enforced through execution proceedings and may even warrant civil detention of the defaulter.
