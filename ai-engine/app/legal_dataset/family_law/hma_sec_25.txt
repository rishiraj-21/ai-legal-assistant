ACT: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955
SECTION: 25
TITLE: Permanent Alimony and Maintenance
CATEGORY: Family Law - Maintenance
---
Section 25 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 empowers the court to grant permanent alimony and maintenance at the time of passing any decree or at any time subsequent thereto. The court may order the respondent to pay to the applicant for maintenance and support such gross sum or monthly or periodical sum for a term not exceeding the life of the applicant as the court deems just, having regard to the respondent's own income and other property, the income and property of the applicant, the conduct of the parties, and other circumstances of the case. The court may modify or rescind the order if there is a change in circumstances, such as a significant change in the income of either party, remarriage of the applicant, or if the applicant has not remained chaste. In Rajnesh v. Neha (2021), the Supreme Court laid down that permanent alimony should not be a windfall for the applicant and must be proportionate to the status and financial condition of both parties. The Supreme Court in Vimla (Smt.) v. Veeraswamy (1991) held that Section 25 entitles the wife to claim maintenance in the form of a lump sum payment or regular monthly payments, and the court has wide discretion in determining the quantum. Factors considered include the duration of the marriage, the age and health of the applicant, the applicant's qualifications and employability, the contribution of the applicant to the family, the standard of living enjoyed during the marriage, and the needs of children. Unlike maintenance pendente lite under Section 24 which is temporary, permanent alimony under Section 25 represents the final financial settlement between the parties. The order for permanent alimony can be enforced as a decree for money, including through attachment and sale of the respondent's property.
