ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 84
TITLE: Liability of Product Manufacturer
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Product Liability
---
Section 84 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 specifies the grounds on which a product manufacturer shall be held liable in a product liability action. A product manufacturer is liable if the product contains a manufacturing defect, meaning it deviates from the intended design or manufacturing specifications even if all possible care was exercised. The manufacturer is also liable if the product is defective in design, meaning that despite proper manufacturing the design itself was unreasonably dangerous. Liability arises if there is a deviation from manufacturing specifications resulting in the product not meeting the quality, safety, or performance standards that a reasonable consumer would expect. The manufacturer is further liable if the product does not conform to an express warranty made by the manufacturer, or if the product fails to contain adequate instructions for correct usage or warnings regarding the potential dangers or hazards of its foreseeable use. The section essentially imposes strict liability on manufacturers for defective products, moving away from the traditional requirement of proving negligence. However, the manufacturer can raise defences including that the product was not defective at the time it left the manufacturer's control, that the injury was caused by misuse or alteration of the product by the consumer, or that the consumer was aware of the defect and voluntarily assumed the risk. The Supreme Court's earlier landmark decision in M.C. Mehta v. Union of India (1987) on absolute liability for hazardous industries provided the judicial foundation for statutory product liability in India. Section 84 read with Section 83 defines the scope of product liability action comprehensively, covering manufacturing defects, design defects, failure to warn, and breach of express warranty.
