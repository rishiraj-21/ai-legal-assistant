ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 2
TITLE: Definitions - Consumer, Deficiency, Defect, Service, Goods
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Definitions
---
Section 2 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 provides comprehensive definitions critical to the functioning of the Act. Section 2(7) defines "consumer" as any person who buys goods or avails services for consideration, including offline and online transactions through electronic means, but does not include a person who obtains goods for resale or for any commercial purpose, except a person who buys goods and uses them exclusively for the purpose of earning his livelihood by means of self-employment. Section 2(11) defines "defect" as any fault, imperfection, or shortcoming in the quality, quantity, potency, purity, or standard which is required to be maintained by or under any law, or which has been claimed by the trader. Section 2(12) defines "deficiency" as any fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in the quality, nature, and manner of performance which is required to be maintained by or under any law or which has been undertaken to be performed by a person in pursuance of a contract. Section 2(42) defines "service" broadly to include any service of any description made available to potential users, including banking, financing, insurance, transport, processing, supply of electrical or other energy, housing construction, entertainment, and amusement, but excludes services rendered free of charge or under a contract of personal service. The 2019 Act significantly expanded the definition of consumer to include e-commerce transactions, reflecting the modern digital marketplace. The concept of "unfair trade practice" under Section 2(47) has been broadened to include misleading advertisements and refusal to withdraw defective goods from the market. These definitions form the jurisdictional foundation for all consumer complaints filed before the District, State, and National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions.
