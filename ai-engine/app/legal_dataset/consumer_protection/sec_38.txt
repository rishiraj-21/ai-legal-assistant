ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 38
TITLE: Procedure on Admission of Complaint
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Procedure
---
Section 38 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 lays down the procedure to be followed by the District Commission upon receipt and admission of a consumer complaint. Upon admission, the District Commission shall refer a copy of the complaint to the opposite party, directing the opposite party to give his version of the case within a period of thirty days or such extended period not exceeding fifteen additional days as may be granted by the Commission. If the opposite party fails to present his version within the stipulated time, the District Commission shall proceed to settle the complaint ex parte on merits. Where the opposite party denies or disputes the allegations, or where the complaint requires testing or analysis of goods, the District Commission may refer the goods for appropriate laboratory testing. The cost of such testing is initially borne by the complainant but may be recovered from the opposite party if the complaint succeeds. The District Commission may also refer the matter to mediation under Chapter V of the Act where there exists a possibility of settlement, provided both parties agree to mediation. The mediation must be completed within forty-five days from the date of referral. If mediation fails, the complaint reverts to the District Commission for adjudication. The proceedings before the District Commission are summary in nature and are governed by the principles of natural justice rather than the strict rules of evidence and civil procedure. The Commission has the powers of a civil court for summoning and enforcing attendance, receiving evidence on affidavits, and requisitioning public records.
