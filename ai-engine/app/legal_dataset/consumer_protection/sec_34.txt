ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 34
TITLE: Jurisdiction of District Commission
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Jurisdiction
---
Section 34 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 defines the jurisdiction of the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission. The District Commission has jurisdiction to entertain complaints where the value of goods or services paid as consideration does not exceed one crore rupees (Rs. 1,00,00,000). This is a significant increase from the previous limit of twenty lakh rupees under the Consumer Protection Act, 1986. The District Commission is established for each district by the State Government through notification and is headed by a President who is or has been or is qualified to be a District Judge, along with not less than two members. Complaints must be filed in the District Commission within whose territorial jurisdiction the opposite party resides or carries on business, or where the cause of action wholly or in part arises. The complainant can also file where the opposite party has a branch office if the branch office is within the district. The District Commission must endeavour to decide complaints within three months from the date of receipt of notice by the opposite party where the complaint does not require analysis or testing of goods, and within five months if such analysis or testing is required. The 2019 Act introduced the provision for e-filing of complaints, making the process more accessible to consumers across the country. Appeals from orders of the District Commission lie before the State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission within a period of 45 days from the date of the order.
