ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 39
TITLE: Finding of the District Commission
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Relief and Remedies
---
Section 39 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 empowers the District Commission to issue appropriate orders upon finding that the goods or services complained against suffer from any defect or deficiency, or that the opposite party has engaged in unfair or restrictive trade practice. The reliefs that the District Commission can grant include: removal of the defect from the goods, replacement of the defective goods with new goods of similar description, return of the price or charges paid by the complainant, payment of such amount as compensation for any loss or injury suffered by the consumer due to the negligence of the opposite party, discontinuation of the unfair or restrictive trade practice, withdrawal of hazardous goods from sale, cessation of manufacture of hazardous goods, payment of adequate costs to the parties, and issuance of corrective advertisements. The Commission may also grant punitive damages in appropriate cases, particularly where the opposite party has been negligent or acted in a manner causing undue hardship to the consumer. The order of the District Commission is enforceable as a decree of a civil court, and non-compliance can result in attachment and sale of property, arrest and detention, and appointment of a receiver. If the opposite party fails to comply with the order, the consumer may apply for execution, and the Commission can punish for contempt. The 2019 Act strengthened the enforcement mechanism by providing specific timelines for compliance and penalties for non-compliance.
