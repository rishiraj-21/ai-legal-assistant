ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 69
TITLE: Product Liability
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Product Liability
---
Section 69 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 introduces the concept of product liability into Indian consumer protection law for the first time. A product liability action may be brought by a complainant against a product manufacturer, product service provider, or product seller for any harm caused to the consumer by a defective product manufactured, sold, or serviced by such person. "Harm" is defined broadly to include damage to property, personal injury including illness or death, mental agony or emotional distress attendant to personal injury or damage to property, and any loss of consortium or services resulting from death. Under Section 84, the product manufacturer is liable if the product contains a manufacturing defect, is defective in design, deviates from manufacturing specifications, does not conform to the express warranty, or fails to contain adequate instructions or warnings regarding the dangerous nature of the product. The product service provider is liable under Section 85 if the service was faulty or imperfect, there was an act of omission or commission amounting to negligence, or the service provider did not issue adequate warnings or instructions. The product seller is liable under Section 86 if the seller exercised substantial control over the design, testing, manufacture, packaging, or labelling of the product, or the seller altered or modified the product, or the seller made an express warranty independent of the manufacturer. Product liability provisions represent a significant shift from the fault-based liability regime to a more consumer-friendly framework. The burden of proof in product liability actions rests on the complainant to show defect and harm, but defences available include assumption of risk, contributory negligence, and product alteration by the consumer.
