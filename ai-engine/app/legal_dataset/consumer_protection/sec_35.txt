ACT: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
SECTION: 35
TITLE: Manner in Which Complaint Shall Be Made
CATEGORY: Consumer Law - Filing of Complaint
---
Section 35 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 prescribes the manner in which a consumer complaint shall be filed before the District Commission. Sub-section (1) provides that every complaint shall be filed with the District Commission by the consumer to whom the goods are sold or delivered or the service provided, any recognized consumer association, one or more consumers having the same interest with the permission of the District Commission on behalf of or for the benefit of all consumers so interested, or the Central Government, the Central Authority, or the State Government. The complaint must contain the name and address of the complainant and opposite party, facts relating to the complaint and when the cause of action arose, documents in support of the allegations, and the relief claimed. The complaint must be filed within two years from the date on which the cause of action has arisen, though the District Commission may condone the delay if sufficient cause is shown. Sub-section (2) mandates that every complaint shall be accompanied by such fee as may be prescribed, which must not exceed the maximum fee specified in the Act. The 2019 Act introduced electronic filing through the Consumer Commission's website, enabling consumers to file complaints online. The complaint can be filed in the form prescribed by the Central Government and must be supported by an affidavit. Where the complainant is an association, it must provide evidence of its registration and authorization to file the complaint. The filing of a complaint does not require engagement of a lawyer, as the consumer forums are designed to be accessible and consumer-friendly.
